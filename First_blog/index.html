How to make Tom Brown cereal from scratch

Wheat porridge is a nutritious breakfast dish made from whole wheat grains that is popular in many cultures around the world. It is known for its ability to provide sustained energy throughout the day and can be topped with a variety of sweet or savory toppings. Wheat porridge is a good source of fiber, vitamins, and minerals and can be customized to suit individual tastes and preferences. Overall, it is a healthy and satisfying breakfast option that can help fuel a productive day ahead.

This healthy powder is highly nutritious and filling, maintaining your baby’s health even after you’ve stopped breastfeeding. It’s one of the popular Ghanian meals used to wean babies.

Benefits of Tom Brown to the human body

Rich in nutrient:
-Wheat porridge is a good source of vitamins and minerals, including iron, calcium, and B vitamins.
High in fiber:
-Wheat porridge contains high amounts of dietary fiber, which can help improve digestion, lower cholesterol levels, and reduce the risk of heart disease.
Low in fat:
-Wheat porridge is a low-fat food that can be a good option for people looking to reduce their overall fat intake.
Provides sustained energy:
-The complex carbohydrates in wheat porridge provide sustained energy, making it a good breakfast option for those looking to stay full and energized throughout the morning.
Versatile:
-Wheat porridge can be prepared in a variety of ways and can be customized with toppings such as fruits, nuts, and seeds for added flavor and nutrition.

Ingredients

  • Millet – 500grams
  • Guinea corn – 1kilogram
  • Yellow corn -1kilogram
  • Soybeans – 500grams
  • Deshelled Groundnut (peanuts) – 200grams

Step By Step Preperation Of Tom Brown Powder

  1. Put your grains in separate trays and carefully remove the stones and foreign particles
  2. After picking out the stones from your grains, wash them separately and spread them out to dry in the sun.
  3. Pack them in separate bowls once they are completely dry.
  4. Toast your corn using a wide frying pan. Be careful not to fry too much to a point where it begins to pop like popcorn.
  5. Also roast your groundnut, millet and soya beans separately till they become golden brown.
  6. After toasting, spread your grains and allow them to cool down.
  7. The next step is to remove the outer shell/ seed coat (also known as chaff) of the soya beans. You can do this by grinding it softy with a mortar and pestle.
  8. When you are done grinding, sift the chaff away by breezing it out. Then pour it into a suitable bowl.
  9. Next thing is to grind your crayfish, ginger and kayan kanshi. You can either use a mortar and pestle for this or you use a blender. Put it inside a bowl after grinding.
  10. Now, you need to grind your dates (dabino) as well. Break them open and remove and discard the seeds inside first. Then crush the outer fruit with a blender or mortar and pestle.
  11. Pour all your ingredients in a bowl and mix them well.
  12. You can blend this mixture with a high power blender at home or you you take it to a local mill to blend. Do not add water while blending.
  13. After blending, pour your powder on a wide tray and allow it to cool down completely.
  14. Next thing is to sieve your mixture to remove any residual chaff. This leaves you with a smoother powder.
  15. Viola! Your tom brown is ready. Just store it inside a sizeable container and prepare it anytime you want.
